FSB Movement
FSB Silver Stands Down — Cat Platoon Last to Leave
FSB Silver → FSB Fontaine, Long Khanh Province
FSB Silver was dismantled and the company relocated to the newly established FSB Fontaine. A letter dated January 30 confirmed the move was complete. Cat Platoon was the last unit to leave Silver. Notably, SGT Marvin Miller was asked by the Colonel to draw a scaled model of the new base at Fontaine — a detail suggesting he had recognized drafting or spatial skills.

1971
Combat Action Multiple Accounts
Contact North of FSB Fanning — Three Killed in Action
North of Route 1, east of Gia Rai Mountain, Long Khanh Province
Delta Company, ordered to patrol north of the nearly-complete FSB Fanning, made contact with a fortified NVA base camp along a major supply trail running parallel to a stream. Range Platoon, crossing to the north bank of the creek, walked into an ambush at a bunker complex. Three soldiers were killed and multiple others severely wounded before the company withdrew, called in artillery and air strikes, and eventually recovered the bodies.

1971
Incident Photographed — Only Known Record
Huey Crash at FSB Fontaine — Pilot, Co-pilot, and SP4 Colburn Killed
FSB Fontaine, Long Khanh Province
A UH-1 Huey departed FSB Fontaine and crashed shortly after takeoff. The pilot, co-pilot, and Battalion Armor SP4 Richard Colburn were killed. Door gunner Nathan Stanfield survived with his arm pinned under the skid. Company Armor SGT James Garvin had been bumped from the flight moments before departure and watched the helicopter go down from the firebase. SGT Marvin Miller, among the first soldiers on scene after casualties were removed, photographed the wreckage through the recovery operation — producing what are believed to be the only known photographs of this incident.

29 Apr
1971
Division Drawdown
1st Cavalry Division Withdraws — D/2-8 Remains
Long Khanh Province, Vietnam
The bulk of the 1st Cavalry Division (Airmobile) departed Vietnam for Fort Hood, Texas — nine days after the April 20 contact north of FSB Fanning. The 3rd Brigade, 1st Cavalry Division (Separate), including the 2nd Battalion, 8th Cavalry, remained in-country as part of the Vietnamization rearguard. D Company, 2/8 Cav was among the final US ground combat units remaining in Vietnam. The battalion continued to operate east of Bien Hoa in Long Khanh Province, standing down in June 1972 — a full year after the main division left. At the time of this drawdown, SGT Marvin Miller had approximately eight months remaining in his tour.
Source Note Division withdrawal date confirmed by 1st Cavalry Division Association records and Wikipedia unit history. Battalion stand-down date (June 1972) confirmed by GlobalSecurity.org unit history for 2nd Battalion, 8th Cavalry.

KIACat at CP
Contact — PFC Bill Makowski KIA
Long Khanh Province (exact location to be confirmed)
PFC Bill Makowski, Range 71, was killed in a firefight. He had been in the field only one month and had just turned 20. During the same engagement, a bullet passed through Doug Hilts' helmet lining leaving him stunned with limited memory of events, and Dizzy Blais sustained shrapnel wounds to his face. Cat Platoon was near Captain Wolf Kutter and the command post during this contact and was not in a forward position to address the ambush — consistent with the pattern observed on April 20, 1971. A written account of this incident by Captain Kutter exists and will be added to this record when accessible. This contact may have been one of Marvin Miller's last field operations before being stood down as a short-timer.
Source Note Primary account: COL Wolf Kutter (Ret.) — document in possession of Marvin Miller's son, not yet transcribed. Supporting detail: 2022 ASA Newsletter (Garvin account of 2021 memorial). Cat Platoon positioning: family recollection of Kutter account. Short-timer context: confirmed by Kutter and Val Romani.

1971
Short-Timer Policy
Stood Down — Battalion HQ for Final Weeks
Battalion Headquarters, Long Khanh Province
Per confirmed policy described by both Captain Kutter and Val Romani, soldiers approaching their DEROS date were not sent to the bush. With his December 2 DEROS approaching, SGT Marvin Miller was stood down from field operations approximately one month before departure and assigned to Battalion HQ. His November 24 letter home described a "short-timer's attitude" and asked his family to stop writing — not just a psychological state, but the literal reality of a soldier in an administrative holding pattern, waiting out his final days in-country. The boredom and quietness of his final letters reflects this period at HQ rather than field operations.
Source Note Short-timer policy confirmed by COL Wolf Kutter (Ret.) and Val Romani. Letter of November 24, 1971 provides Marvin's own description of this period. Approximate stand-down date estimated from DEROS of December 2, 1971.
2 Dec
1971
Departure
SGT Marvin Miller Departs — Honorable Discharge
US Army Personnel Center, Oakland, California
SGT Marvin Dale Miller, Cat Platoon, was honorably discharged after exactly one year in-country. In his final letter home, dated November 24, he had described having a "short-timer's attitude" and asked family to stop writing. He returned to Kittanning, Armstrong County, Pennsylvania. He passed away in 2004 without discussing his service in detail with his family. Ken Weaver's photographs from a going-away party held in the rear in mid-December 1971 do not include Marvin — he was already home in Kittanning when the party took place. Weaver himself departed shortly before Christmas 1971, appearing in those photographs as one of the men being sent off.
