## Summary

On May 10, 1972, a Chinook helicopter carrying Skull Platoon of D Company, 2/8 Cavalry crashed, killing approximately 29 soldiers and 5 crew members. The company commander, Capt. Ken Rosenberg, was among those killed. Skull Platoon was effectively destroyed in its entirety. The crash was photographed from the ground by Roy Adams of Cat Platoon, who was not aboard the aircraft.

The cause of the crash — mechanical failure or hostile fire — was disputed in the immediate aftermath. Mechanical failure is the accepted account in oral history sources available to this archive, but no formal accident investigation result has been obtained.

## Context and Contested Details

**Cause of crash:** Initial accounts suggested hostile fire. Subsequent unit history attributes the crash to mechanical failure. The formal Army accident investigation result has not been located. This record treats mechanical failure as the working explanation while noting the initial uncertainty.

**Scale of loss:** The loss of an entire platoon in a single event is the largest single-incident casualty loss in the archive. Individual names for most of the 29 soldiers have not yet been entered into the archive — this is a known and significant gap.

**Roy Adams as witness:** Adams's photographs are the primary visual record of this event. His position on the ground places him as a witness to the crash site rather than a survivor.
