### Fall Semester 1969
**Enrolled — Art Institute of Pittsburgh, Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania**
One semester only. Enrolled with at least partial motivation of obtaining
a student deferment from the draft, which was available under the rules
in effect before the lottery system replaced it.

Completed one full semester. Did not return for a second semester after
the December 1969 lottery announcement made it clear the deferment path
was closing.

Significance: This training later distinguished Marvin in the field.
When FSB Fontaine was established in late January 1971, he was asked by
the Colonel to draw a scaled model of the new firebase — a request made
specifically because his artistic training had been noted. His
photography during the tour also reflects formal compositional awareness.
Confidence: FAMILY (one semester duration confirmed)

### ~August / September 1970
**Selected for NCO Candidate Course (NCOC) — Fort Benning, Georgia**
Known informally as the "Shake and Bake" program — an accelerated NCO
development course that produced junior sergeants (E-5) in 12 weeks,
bypassing the traditional years of service normally required for promotion.
The program was controversial but necessary given the high casualty rate
among junior NCOs in Vietnam.

**Marvin did not complete the course.** He withdrew or was removed at some
point during the program. The specific circumstances are known to the family
but are recorded separately.

### ~October / November 1970
**Possible reassignment — Fort Hood, Texas, or held at Fort Benning**
Following non-completion of the NCOC at Fort Benning, Marvin was
reassigned through the Army's personnel system to await Vietnam orders.

**Note on Fort Hood:** Family recollection places Marvin at Fort Hood
briefly before Vietnam, but this is worth treating carefully. The 1st
Cavalry Division — the unit Marvin was assigned to in Vietnam — did not
return to Fort Hood until April 29, 1971, six months after his December
1970 arrival in-country. In late 1970 the 1st Cav was entirely in Vietnam.
Fort Hood was not yet the 1st Cav's home station during his training
pipeline, so a connection through that route is unlikely.

Fort Hood was however one of the Army's largest installations and a
general staging and processing point for soldiers awaiting overseas
orders, regardless of unit assignment. A brief hold there is plausible
on those grounds alone.

An alternative: soldiers who washed out of the NCOC at Fort Benning were
sometimes held at Benning itself for reassignment processing. It's possible
Marvin's time between NCOC and Vietnam orders was spent at Benning rather
than Hood, or split between the two.

**The DD-214 duty station entries would resolve this definitively.**
Confidence: FAMILY (Fort Hood mentioned); UNCERTAIN (reason for Fort Hood
and duration); ESTIMATED (dates)

## Records Request Note

The National Personnel Records Center (NPRC) in St. Louis holds service
records for veterans. As next of kin of a deceased veteran, Marvin's son
is entitled to request his full service record (not just the DD-214),
which would include:
- Training records showing exact BCT and AIT graduation dates
- Assignment history showing dates at each duty station
- NCOC enrollment and separation record
- Travel orders showing port of embarkation

**Request via:** nprc.archives.gov
**Form:** SF-180 (Request Pertaining to Military Records)
**Note:** Allow 3–6 months for response. Records for Vietnam-era
veterans were partially destroyed in the 1973 NPRC fire, but training
records and assignment histories were often separately maintained and
may survive.
