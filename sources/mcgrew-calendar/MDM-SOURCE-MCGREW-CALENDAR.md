# Howard McGrew — In-Country Operational Calendar, 1971
## Archive ID: MDM-SOURCE-MCGREW-CALENDAR
## Source type: Primary source — contemporary field calendar
## Keeper: Howard McGrew, Range Platoon, D/2-8 Cav, 70-71
## Shared with: Son of SGT Marvin Miller, 2024-2025
## Transcribed: April 2026

---

### Significance

This calendar is one of the most important documents in the D/2-8 archive.
It provides a day-by-day operational record of Delta Company's movements,
missions, contacts, R&R periods, and administrative events throughout 1971
— written in the field, in real time, by a soldier who was there.

It independently confirms, corrects, and expands the movement timeline
previously compiled from Marvin Miller's letters and veteran accounts.
Every entry is a contemporary primary source. Cross-references to other
archive documents are noted throughout.

**Howard McGrew** is listed in the 2022 ASA Newsletter as Range 70-71.
He attended the April 20, 2021 memorial for Danny Drinkard at White Chapel
Cemetery, Troy MI. He is a confirmed active contact.

**Abbreviations used by McGrew:**
- CA = Combat Assault (helicopter insertion)
- SA = Single Aircraft (as opposed to full combat assault)
- FB / FSB = Fire Support Base
- SB = Support Base (variant of FSB)
- Loc = Location
- New Loc = New location / position
- Jeff / Jeffries = FSB Jeffries
- Font / Fontaine = FSB Fontaine
- Mace = FSB Mace
- Silver = FSB Silver
- Fanning = FSB Fanning
- King = FSB King
- R&R = Rest and Recuperation (leave)
- DEROS = Date Eligible for Return from Overseas
- ETS = Expiration of Term of Service
- 90th Repl = 90th Replacement Battalion (out-processing center)
- Chuck Chuck = likely "Chinook" (CH-47 helicopter)
- ARUN = unclear — possibly a base or unit designation

| Date | Entry | Notes |

| Apr 13 Wed | Returned to Fontaine | Back at FSB Fontaine |
| Apr 19 Mon | CA to Mission | Operation — the day before the contact |
| **Apr 20 Tues** | **Contact** | **Written in bolder/larger script than surrounding entries. THE April 20, 1971 contact. Cardwell, Drinkard, Hall KIA. Sargent mortally wounded.** |
| Apr 23 Fri | CA to Fontaine | Return to Fontaine — 3 days after the contact |
| **Apr 24 Sat** | **Downed Chopper** | **The Huey crash at FSB Fontaine. Fanning, Jeffries, Colburn KIA.** |
| Apr 26 Mon | CA to Mission | Operations resumed 2 days after the crash |
| Apr 28 Wed | CA to New Loc | Position change |

**Cross-references:** April 20 and April 24 are both documented here in McGrew's own hand, written at the time. The entries are brief — "Contact" and "Downed Chopper" — but their presence in a contemporaneous calendar is significant corroboration. Operations resumed April 26, just two days after the crash.

**Critical finding:** The calendar shows the unit was back on missions within 48 hours of the April 24 crash. The "sent to the rear" period that family recollection and the May 22 letter suggest may refer to a later date, or may have applied to some elements of the company while others continued operations. This warrants clarification with Weaver or other veterans.
