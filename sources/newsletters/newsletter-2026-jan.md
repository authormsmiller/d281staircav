## Story 1: How an Army Tank Almost Sank a Navy Destroyer
**Contributor: Bill Bott, Range 6–1971**
**Additional: Jim Garvin (The Rest of the Story)**

In May 1971 Range Platoon heard movement outside the perimeter one night and called in cover fire. Artillery FO SGT Steve "Tank" Kahnke began calling fire from the USS Larson — a Gearing Class US Navy destroyer launched January 28, 1945, stationed in the South China Sea.

As the fire mission progressed, Kahnke was impressed with the accuracy and kept walking in the rounds. Finally he called for a "fire for effect." 

A day or two later the Navy suggested a personnel exchange. Captain Neal asked for a volunteer and Lt. Bott agreed. Tom Emery, Jessy Freeman, and Joe Kent also went out to the ship. (Tom Emery took photographs May 29, 1971 while aboard the Larson.) Navy personnel came to the FSB, were taken on patrol, and participated in a Mad Minute.

On the ship, the grunts were given a tour and escorted to the galley for turkey with mashed potatoes and gravy. Bott sat with a Navy man who eventually learned Bott was an officer and asked "Should I call you sir?" — Bott was then escorted to the Captain's Mess and expected to pay for a second meal.

The Captain of the Larson told them that because of the age of the ship and the rounds fired during fire for effect, "We now have leaks we've never had before."

*Note: Bott thought he better not tell them they were possibly shooting at Apes and not the enemy.*

**USS Larson details:** Commissioned April 6, 1945 — Decommissioned August 1972 — Transferred to South Korea October 30, 1972.

**Continuation (Jim Garvin, "The Rest of the Story"):**

Fast forward 50 years to June 12, 2021. Jim Garvin and his wife Susan visit his niece and sister Muriel and her husband Dick Petrowich in Memphis, TN. Dick had served in the Navy March 1954 – May 1957 on the USS Shelton (another Gearing class Destroyer, launched March 8, 1946) during the Korean War. Dick and Muriel had been attending USS Shelton reunions since 1990 and in 2000 took over newsletter and reunion planning. In 2001 the reunion was held in Great Falls, Montana with 32 members of the Shelton family.

In Memphis, Dick mentioned the declining numbers of Shelton survivors and how they'd invited Gearing class ships including the Larson to join a 2021 reunion. This reminded Garvin of Bott's story. He sent Tom Emery's photos to Dick who forwarded them to the Larson contact. Dick identified Ellis WarmKessel in one of the photos — a Larson sailor Dick and Muriel had previously met at a Larson reunion.

March 11, 2025: Dick Petrowich passed away. Jim and Susan attended the memorial. A man named Dennis Dunn, who served on yet another Gearing class destroyer USS Eversole, urged Jim to bring Muriel to the Gearing Gang Reunion in Buffalo NY in September.

## Story 2: The Dump
**Contributor: Ed Regan, Range One, Summer 1968**

Range One squad assigned to guard a dump between Highway 1 in Quang Tri and LZ Betty (Brigade HQ, 1st Cav). Orders: "Keep the kids out." The squad used up a case of CS grenades trying. A weird colored cloud appeared just as the Division Commander was visiting — everyone started retching, the General grabbed the Colonel's gas mask, and lieutenants were left puking. No one ever got CS gas again.

## Story 3: Burn Pits
**Contributor: Stanley A. Dillon**

Documents the routine burning of human waste and medical waste at fire support bases. The most dreaded duty returning from the jungle was managing burn pits — standing over burning waste mixed with diesel or JP-8 fuel, stirring to ensure combustion, breathing thick acrid smoke.

Specifically mentions Jack T. Langan returning from the jungle to manage burn pits at the base.

Contrary to the misconception that burn pit exposure is only a post-9/11 concern, Vietnam fire base soldiers were regularly exposed to these emissions. The cumulative exposure has had lasting effects on many veterans.

*Note: Dillon references this in context of VA claims and health advocacy.*
