## Foreword: Col. Wolf Kutter (Ret.), Skipper 6 1971–1972

Describes the Never Forgotten memorial program as moving and powerful. Notes that 50 years after the ambush of April 20, 1971, the Association honored fallen brothers and their families. Howard McGrew (Range 71) attended Danny Drinkard's memorial and described how it "brought family and friends together in ways that had to be witnessed in person to understand."

Kutter writes: "By seizing this powerful moment — half a century later — we are leading the way for the next generation to appreciate the sacrifices not only of our fallen, but also the sorrow and the memories never created with their immediate Gold Star families."

## Never Forgotten — 2021 Memorial Program
**By Jim Garvin, Range 70–71**

### Danny Drinkard Memorial — April 20, 2021, Troy MI

50 years to the day of the April 20, 1971 ambush, Stan Dillon organized a visit to Danny Drinkard's grave at White Chapel Cemetery, Troy MI. 18 Angry Skippers and wives attended.

**Attendees:** Stan & Gail Dillon, Frank Guidara (Range 70-71), Bill & Mary Alice Bott (Range 70-71), Ed Regan (Range 68-69), Al & Cathy Seal (Range 71), Doug & Barb Hilts (Range 70-71), Kirk Davis (Range 70), Howard & Beverly McGrew (Range 70-71), John Lee (Range 68-69), Phil & Rowena Leekley (Cat 72), Jim & Susan Garvin.

More than 50 people attended in the snow. A young man named Steve O'Donnell had been tending Danny's grave for 15 years — his own uncle was killed in Vietnam on April 20, 1970, exactly one year before Danny. He had researched Danny and felt a connection. O'Donnell was at the cemetery that day paying respects to his uncle and had been found by Howard McGrew.

**Confirmed KIA from April 20, 1971:** Danny Drinkard (Range 71), Joe Hall (Range 70-71), James Cardwell (Range 70-71), Stan Sargent (Range 70-71) — died of wounds the following day.

### Craig Waterman Memorial — May 22, 2021, Rockwell City IA

Craig Waterman (Range 70) was killed in a friendly fire incident September 3, 1970. Rich Hanson had always wanted to visit Craig's grave.

**Attendees:** Stan & Gail Dillon, Phil & Rowena Leekley, Jim & Susan Garvin.

The local community had honored Craig: American flag on fire department ladder truck, county museum holding family scrapbooks and shadow box of service medals, flagpole erected by his 1969 graduating class.

An older man rode a motorized wheelchair in the rain several miles to attend. Phil Leekley (Cat) read a moving email from Rich Aulenbach describing his memories of Craig's death.

The Calhoun County Museum and local AmVets received memorial posters. Commander George Sexton told them the post had petitioned national AmVets to rename their post in Craig's honor.

Rich Hanson, unable to attend due to illness, received a visit from Jim and Susan Garvin the following day — they found Craig's nephew Joe Moore had driven 200+ miles to shake Rich's hand. Rich passed away June 17, 2021, less than a month later.

### Bill Makowski Memorial — October 21, 2021, Buffalo NY

Bill Makowski (Range 71) was killed October 21, 1971 — in the field only one month, just turned 20. Doug Hilts later realized a bullet had traveled through his helmet lining during the same firefight. Dizzy Blais sustained shrapnel wounds.

**Attendees:** Kirk Davis, Wolf & Tracie Kutter, Dizzy & Nancy Blais, Doug & Barb Hilts, Jim & Susan Garvin.

Approximately 40 attended. WIVB News 4 Buffalo covered the event. Pat Kavanaugh, archivist at Forest Lawn Cemetery and Vietnam veteran, arranged TV coverage. American Legion Post #1626 Honor Guard attended.

Bill had left for Vietnam "with the strong premonition that he would not be coming back alive."

Barbara Hilts handmade memorial quilts for all four families. Doug Hilts presented the quilt; Kirk Davis created memorial posters for each.

*Note: FSB Makowski was named in Bill's honor.*

### Richard Colburn Memorial — October 22, 2021, Sandy Creek NY

SGT Richard Colburn (HHC 69-71) was the Battalion Armor who came to FSB Fontaine on April 24, 1971 to help repair weapons after the April 20 ambush. He was killed when the Huey he boarded crashed on departure.

Jim Garvin's full account: He first met Richard in April 1971, just days after being assigned as D Co Armorer. Richard was on his 2nd tour — first served in 4th Inf. Div., then Recon Platoon of Echo Co., 1st Bn 35th Inf. where he saw heavy combat. During 2nd tour assigned to 1st Cav as Supply SGT and Bn Armorer.

Shortly after the April 20 ambush, Garvin traveled to Fontaine to help D Co. On April 24, overwhelmed by the massive weapons repair job, he looked up to find Richard standing there with W.J. Brooks from 27th Maintenance Bn. They had left Bien Hoa to come help.

Late that evening, as Garvin was expressing deep gratitude, the loadmaster (identified as Tom Vollmar, Skull 70-71) told Garvin he'd been bumped from the flight. Garvin watched as Colburn and Brooks climbed aboard and the helicopter took off — and then it fell out of the sky. The pilot, co-pilot, and Richard Colburn perished.

**"My own survivor's guilt has reminded me for 50 years that I should have been sitting next to him on that bird."**

**Memorial attendees:** Wolf Kutter, Doug Hilts, Dizzy Blais, Kirk Davis, Jim Garvin.

Started with a flag ceremony at the Kendall War Memorial, designed by Eagle Scout Troop #94. Colburn's sisters Donna Tucker and Lisa Colburn Kennedy received the folded US flag. Dinner at Hamlin VFW Hall, 40+ family, friends, and vets.
