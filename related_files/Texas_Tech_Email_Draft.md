Dear Vietnam Center and Archive Staff,

I am writing to introduce myself and to ask for your guidance on two related matters — one as a researcher, and one as a potential contributor to your collections.

I am the son of SGT Marvin Dale Miller (Cat Platoon, D/2-8 Cav, 1st Cavalry Division, December 1970 – December 1971), who passed away in 2004 without discussing his service in detail. Over the past year I have been reconstructing his story through primary sources and, in the process, have assembled what I believe is a significant collection of materials relating to Delta Company, 2nd Battalion, 8th Cavalry Regiment during the 1970–72 period.

The collection currently includes:

— My father's DD-214, letters home (approximately 20 handwritten letters, December 1970 – November 1971), and 50–60 original photographs he took in Vietnam, including what appear to be the only known photographs of the April 24, 1971 Huey crash at FSB Fontaine that killed Captain Martin V. Fanning, WO1 Gabriel A. Jeffries Jr., and SP4 Richard Colburn (both FSBs Fanning and Jeffries were subsequently named for the pilot and co-pilot).

— Written depositions from Captain Bill Neal (Company Commander, Skipper 6, 1971) and SSgt Stan Dillon (Range Platoon Sergeant) describing the April 20, 1971 contact north of FSB Fanning in which three soldiers were killed and several severely wounded.

— A reconstructed movement timeline for Delta Company, December 1970 – December 1971, compiled from my father's letters, which documents the company's presence at FSBs Mace, Silver, Fontaine, Fanning, King, and Jeffries, as well as R&R periods and operations. Several of these firebases (King and Jeffries in particular) do not appear in the official unit record as currently available online.

— A full archive of the Angry Skipper Association's 40 newsletters (1988–2026), which contain first-person accounts, memorial records, and unit history for D/2-8 Cav across the full Vietnam period.

I am in the early stages of building a comprehensive digital archive for D/2-8 Delta Company — a clearinghouse that would aggregate materials from the existing Angry Skipper Association website, Jim Garvin's Then & Now site, and primary sources held by veterans and families. Before committing to a technical architecture, I wanted to reach out to you for two reasons:

First, I would welcome your guidance on what records you currently hold for 2/8 Cav and for the 1st Cavalry Division's 3rd Brigade 1970–72 period. I am aware that your Virtual Vietnam Archive includes the 1st Battalion, 8th Cavalry Association Collection (Collection 369), but I am uncertain whether 2nd Battalion materials are represented.

Second, I would like to understand whether the materials I am assembling — including digitized letters, photographs, depositions, and the compiled timeline — would be of interest to your archive as a donation or deposit, either now or when the digitization work is complete. I want to ensure that whatever is preserved locally is also held somewhere with institutional permanence.

I recognize this is a significant scope of materials and I am not asking you to commit to anything at this stage. I simply want to understand what you hold, what you need, and whether a working relationship makes sense before I make decisions about how to structure and store this collection.

Thank you for the work your archive does. For families like mine — trying to reconstruct a history our fathers chose not to share — it is genuinely important.
