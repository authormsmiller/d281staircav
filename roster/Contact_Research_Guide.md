# D/2-8 Cav Contact Research Guide
## Finding Current Information on Aging Veterans

*Compiled April 2026 — for use with Cat Platoon roster and related contacts*

---

## The Core Problem

Most of these men are 74–82 years old. This means:
- Phone numbers change frequently (landlines dropped, cell phones changed)
- Addresses change as people downsize, move to assisted living, or relocate near family
- Some have died recently without appearing in your current records
- The people-search databases (Spokeo, Whitepages, etc.) are often **3–5 years out of date** for this age group
- Family members are frequently better contact points than the veteran directly

**Always check for an obituary first.** It takes 30 seconds and saves wasted outreach to someone who has passed.

---

## The Research Stack — In Order

Work through these in sequence for each contact. Stop when you have a confirmed current phone or address.

### 1. Obituary Check (30 seconds — do this first)
**Why:** Confirms whether the person is still living before you invest time.

Search terms that work:
```
"[Full Name]" obituary [city] [state]
"[First Name] [Last Name]" obituary [year range: 2020-2026]
```

Best sites:
- **Legacy.com** — aggregates from most US newspapers
- **FindAGrave.com** — often has photos and family notes
- **Dignity Memorial** — funeral home network, good for recent deaths
- Local funeral home websites (search "[city] funeral home obituaries")

If you find an obituary, note:
- Surviving children (by name and city — these become your new contacts)
- VFW/VVA chapter memberships (fellow members may have contact info)
- Date of death for the spreadsheet

---

### 2. Facebook — Veteran Groups
**Why:** Many of these men or their families are active in 1st Cav Facebook groups, often more current than any database.

Search directly on Facebook for:
- `D/2-8 Cavalry` or `2nd Battalion 8th Cavalry Vietnam`
- `1st Cavalry Division Vietnam veterans`
- The soldier's name directly

The D/2-8 Cav group that Jim Garvin has been using is the primary one — Ken Weaver is in it, others may be as well. If you're not already in this group, joining and posting is one of the fastest ways to reach people.

---

### 3. Together We Served
**URL:** `army.togetherweserved.com`

Specifically built for veteran-to-veteran reconnection. Many Vietnam-era Army veterans have profiles here. Search by name and unit (1st Cavalry Division, D/2-8 Cav). Free to browse profiles; some contact features require registration.

---

### 4. White Pages / People Search
Use these for current phone/address, but **verify with a second source before calling** — data is often stale.

Best options in order:
- **WhitePages.com** — generally more current than Spokeo
- **Spokeo.com** — useful for relative names (helps find next of kin)
- **Radaris.com** — sometimes has more granular location data
- **TruePeopleSearch.com** — free, surprisingly current

Search tip: Always search **[Last Name] [First Name] [State]** rather than full name — gives cleaner results for common names.

---

### 5. County Property Records
**Why:** These update when someone buys or sells a home — more current than people-search databases for recent moves.

Most counties have free online property search. Search `[county name] county property records` or `[county name] appraisal district`.

If you know what county someone lives in (from their last known address), search their name in that county's records. If they've moved, the new owner's name will show — and sometimes the forwarding address is on file.

---

### 6. VFW / VVA Chapter Locator
If a veteran is listed as a member of a local VFW or VVA chapter (sometimes visible in obituaries or Radaris), the chapter commander often knows current contact information for members.

- **VFW Post Locator:** `vfw.org/find-a-post`
- **VVA Chapter Locator:** `vva.org/find-a-chapter`

Call the post/chapter directly and explain you're researching D/2-8 Cav unit history. They are generally very willing to help.

---

### 7. LinkedIn
Surprisingly useful for veterans who had post-service careers in government, business, or education. Search `[Full Name] Vietnam veteran` or just the name plus their known city.

---

### 8. Newspapers.com / Local News Archives
For someone like John Steelman (City Manager of El Campo), local newspaper archives may have:
- Retirement announcements
- Community involvement articles
- Letters to the editor
- Recent mentions that confirm current location

Search `[Name] "El Campo" site:eldailynews.com` or the local paper's website directly.

## General Search Query Templates

**Living status check:**
```
"[First Name] [Last Name]" obituary [state] [2018 OR 2019 OR 2020 OR 2021 OR 2022 OR 2023 OR 2024 OR 2025]
```

**Current location:**
```
"[First Name] [Last Name]" [last known city] [state] [current year]
```

**Veteran-specific:**
```
"[First Name] [Last Name]" "1st Cavalry" OR "1st Cav" Vietnam
"[First Name] [Last Name]" veteran [state]
```

**Family contact (when veteran is deceased):**
Search the obituary for children's names, then search:
```
"[Child's Name]" [city from obituary]
```

**Local newspaper:**
```
"[Full Name]" site:[local paper domain]
```

## Notes on the National Archives Research

Based on our search, the unit records for 2/8 Cav 1970–1971 exist in **Record Group 472** at the National Archives at College Park, MD. These include:
- S2/S3 daily journals (most granular — grid locations, contact reports)
- After-action reports
- Unit histories
- Organizational records

These are **not yet digitized** for the 1971 period. Options:
1. **Email inquiry to NARA History Hub** (`historyhub.history.gov`) — they will confirm what boxes exist for 2/8 Cav 1971 and what they contain
2. **Vietnam Center and Archive, Texas Tech** — email their reference staff at `vva@ttu.edu`; they have the 1st Cav Collection and may have 2/8 material
3. **In-person visit to College Park** — researcher card required, can be arranged online

The April 20, 1971 contact would appear in: the S3 daily journal for that date, and likely an after-action report filed within days. These would provide grid coordinates (allowing the site to be identified on modern maps), unit disposition, and the official casualty record.
